Anthony Cuda Died: A Lifelong Hero and Forxwall EMS Chief Gone

Publish date: 2024-08-22

It is with heavy hearts that we report the passing of a cherished member of the Pittsburgh community, Anthony Cuda, Foxwall EMS Chief. Known for his dedication to public service, Cuda’s sudden death has left a profound void in Pittsburgh and especially within the emergency services community.

A Life of Service

Anthony Cuda dedicated his life to serving the community as a first responder. As the Chief of Foxwall EMS, he was instrumental in shaping the organization’s operations and ensuring that the team was always ready to respond to emergencies. His commitment to his role was evident in his tireless efforts to improve the quality of emergency services in Pittsburgh.

An Unforgettable Legacy

Cuda’s legacy extends beyond his professional contributions. He was known for his compassionate nature and his unwavering dedication to helping others. His colleagues remember him as a mentor who was always willing to share his knowledge and experience.

His loss is deeply felt by the Foxwall EMS team, many of whom credit him with inspiring their careers in emergency services. His impact on this team and the broader Pittsburgh community cannot be overstated.

Honoring Anthony Cuda

As we grieve the loss of Anthony Cuda, it’s important to honor his memory by continuing his legacy of service. This includes supporting the Foxwall EMS team as they navigate this difficult time and ensuring that the high standards of care that Cuda championed are upheld.

It’s also crucial to extend our support to his family, who have shared him with the community for so many years. Our thoughts are with them during this challenging time.

The passing of Anthony Cuda is a significant loss for the Pittsburgh community. As the Foxwall EMS Chief, he played a vital role in safeguarding the well-being of our community members. His dedication to service, his leadership, and his compassionate nature will be sorely missed. Let’s honor his memory by embodying the values he upheld and extending our support to those he left behind.
