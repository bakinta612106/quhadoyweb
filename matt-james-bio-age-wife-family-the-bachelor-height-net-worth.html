Matt James Biography

Matt James is a renowned American television personality, businessman, and former NCAA football player who is best known for being the first male African-American lead for The Bachelor in Season 25. James is a real estate broker, entrepreneur, and community organization founder on his journey to find love for the first time.

James, a North Carolina native graduated from Sanderson High School in 2010. He then attended Wake Forest University where he majored in economics and played wide receiver for the football team. In addition, Matt played professional football, before moving to New York City, where he now resides.

In 2015, Matt had mini-camp tryouts with the Carolina Panthers and New Orleans Saints of the National Football League. Before he relocated to New York, James worked at PNC Bank in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. He currently works for CBRE Group Inc., a commercial real estate company in New York City. In addition, he is the founder of ABC Food Tours, a non-profit organization that educates children in underserved communities about food and exercise in New York City.

Matt James The Bachelor

James and Tyler Cameron, a contestant at The Bachelorette, are friends, work colleagues, and played football together at Wake Forest University. Cameron’s mother nominated James to be a contestant on The Bachelorette before she passed away. Matt was originally set to cast on Season 16 of The Bachelorette, starring Clare Crawley. However, the filming was delayed by the COVID-19 pandemic, and he was instead selected to be the next Bachelor for season 25 of The Bachelor.

By appearing on the 25th season of The Bachelor, Matt became the first male African-American lead in the franchise’s history. He is the first black Bachelor lead in the show’s history. The season ended with James choosing to pursue a relationship with 24-year old graphic designer Rachael Kirkconnell.

In September 2021, he was announced as one of the celebrities competing on season 30 of Dancing with the Stars. He was partnered with Lindsay Arnold but later eliminated on week 4, finishing in the 12th position.
