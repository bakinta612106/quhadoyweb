Whats new on Disney Plus in December 2022!

Publish date: 2024-08-17

This December, subscribers to Disney Plus can look forward to a plethora of new original content. Disney’s streaming service covers you whether you’re looking for new originals set in the MCU or a galaxy far away. The new season of Disney Plus’s original series National Treasure: Edge of History will premiere in December, bringing a new cast and a new set of mysteries to unravel. Idina Menzel: Which Way to the Stage? If These Walls Could Sing and Encanto at the Hollywood Bowl are just a few of the music-themed specials coming our way. So, let’s see the top ten shows on Disney Plus in December 2022.

10. Port Protection Alaska

People far above the Lower 48 are profiled in “Port Protection Alaska.” Port Protection is an isolated town on Alaska’s Prince of Wales Island, located northwest of the island and surrounded by the North Pacific. There are no roads, governments, or law enforcement in the area, so the roughly one hundred people who make it their home push the limits of survival every day. But they believe the stakes are worth it to create a world free of bureaucracy that is both beautiful and safe for its inhabitants.

9. Beyond Magic with DMC 

Sorcery and witchcraft are among the earliest types of magic, and DMC delves into their shadowy and unsettling underworld. DMC’s conception of what magic is capable of is tested to its limits when she travels to a realm where people think the rules of nature can be bent and the undead brought back to life. Drummond Money-Coutts is a world-renowned magician who performs his signature brand of card tricks, deception, and magic at venues worldwide.

8. A Very Backstreet Holiday

Very Backstreet Christmas is the Backstreet Boys’ first Christmas album and their tenth studio album overall (ninth in the United States). Scheduled for release in 2021, the COVID-19 pandemic forced a postponement until October 14, 2022. It debuted at number 17th on the Billboard 200 and number one on the Billboard Top Holiday Albums list. The Backstreet Boys confirmed its release during an episode of Watch What Happened Live! in February 2020 and said they were in talks with their record label.

7. The Great Christmas Light Fight 

Taniya Nayak and Carter Oosterhouse travel the country to evaluate magnificent holiday decorations set up by ordinary people. In each episode, four families chosen after a nationwide search for their impressive Christmas light displays, choreography, and extravagant designs compete to make their homes the ultimate display of holiday cheer. After a winner has been selected, the panel of judges will travel back to their house to personally deliver the cash award of $50,000.

6. The Owl House 

It premiered on Disney Channel on January 10, 2020, and was created by Dana Terrace. The Owl House is an American fantasy television series. The series follows Luz Noceda, a teenage human girl of Dominican and American descent, as she travels through a portal to the Demon Realm and meets the rebellious witch Eda Cawthorne, also known as “The Owl Lady.” Her adorable demon housemate King on the Boiling Isles, an archipelago developed from the remains of a dead titan. Luz finds a new family in an unexpected place while serving as Eda’s apprentice at the Owl House, even though she has no magical abilities.

5. Shark vs. Tuna

Observe a showdown between sea monsters in the clear, blue waters of Ascension Island’s battlegrounds. The distant Ascension Island battlefields saw a fight of true oceanic titans. Most of the time, tuna can outrun and outgrow the sharks in terms of speed, fitness, and size.

4. Heroes of the Mediterranean

The degradation of the Mediterranean Sea over the past 30 years was witnessed by marine biologist Manu San Félix, who sets out on a journey across its waters in search of solutions to the alarming situation and the prevention of more dire consequences.

3. Patagonia Wings

As part of the French High Mountain Military Group’s Patagonia mission, writer Sylvain Tesson is also present. They are all mountaineers at heart and have decided to climb the Fitz Roy range and make a parachute jump from the highest peak to honor the Aéropostale heroes Mermoz, Saint-Exupéry, and Guillaumet.

2. Drain the Oceans

Australian and British documentary series Drain the Oceans debuted on National Geographic on May 28, 2018. The 25-episode documentary series, hosted by Russell Boulter, delves into shipwrecks, treasure, and sunken cities via underwater scanning systems, scientific data, and artistic digital recreations. Andrew Ogilvie of Electric Pictures and Crispin Sadler of Mallinson Sadler Productions for National Geographic produced the show.

1. Alice’s Wonderland Bakery

Based on the classic Disney cartoon from 1951, Alice’s Wonderland Bakery is a popular American computer-animated TV series. This series follows Alice, the great-granddaughter of the real Alice Pleasance Liddell, as she begins her career as a baker at the Wonderland Bakery. She takes the white rabbit Fergie, the cat Hattie, and the Princess of Hearts, Rosa, with her on her culinary adventures across the kingdom.
