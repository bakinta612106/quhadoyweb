Who Will Be in Arcane Season 2?

All the major characters from Arcane Season One, such as Jinx/Powder, Vi, and Caitlyn, will all be making a comeback to Arcane Season 2. The fate of the likes of Jayce, Viktor, and Mel, however, hangs in the balance as they had a rocket fired at them in the ending scene of season one. It is not yet clear who survives and who bites the dust.

It has also been revealed that three new champions from the League of Legends game will be introduced in Arcane Season 2. Guesses have already been made, and one name that is frequently thrown up is Warwick. Warwick is a monster who hunts the gray alleys of the impoverished city of Zaun. Arcane Season 2 Plot

Arcane Season 2 will start off some years from where season one left off, and we are likely to witness the intensification of the rivalry and battle between two siblings, Vi and Jinx/Powder. These two young ladies previously loved each other and fought hard for their impoverished Zaun city.

However, due to some misunderstanding, Powder thinks that Vi abandoned her. She is taken to the richer city of Piltover and thus forms a formidable enemy for Zaun and Vi. In the final episode of season one, Powder and Vi go head to head after Powder kidnaps Vi.

They both survive the encounter, but Powder fires a rocket at a meeting where Piltover votes to give Zaun independence. This is likely to worsen the battle between her and her sister. It may also lead to a full-blown battle between the two cities in Arcane Season 2.

Where Can I Watch Arcane Season 2?

Audiences in the Americas, Europe, and Africa can watch Arcane Season 2 on Netflix. The streaming platform is the original production network for the show, and as such, they exclusively show it. You can also be able to watch Arcane Season 2 on Tencent, a Chinese streaming platform that is the 4th largest streaming platform in the world.

The first episode of Arcane season one was also streamed live on Twitch as part of promotional efforts. This same strategy may also be repeated in season two, meaning that fans may get to watch it there. Meanwhile, if you would like to catch up on Arcane season one as you wait for season two, then head on over to Netflix and Tencent.
